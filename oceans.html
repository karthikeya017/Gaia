PACIFIC OCEAN

  1. Largest and deepest ocean, covering over 63 million sq km..
  2. Divided into North and South Pacific by the equator..
  3. Named "Pacific" by Magellan, meaning peaceful.
  4. Contains the Mariana Trench, Earth's deepest point..
  5. Affects global climate and rich in marine life.



ATLANTIC OCEAN

  1. Second-largest ocean, stretching between the Americas, Europe, and Africa
  2. Known for the Mid-Atlantic Ridge, an underwater mountain range
  3. Important for transatlantic trade and historical exploration routes.
  4. Home to the Sargasso Sea, the only sea without a land boundary
  5. Rich in marine species and significant oil and gas reserves.



INDIAN OCEAN

  1. Third-largest ocean, located mainly in the Southern Hemispher
  2. Bordered by Africa, Asia, Australia, and the Indian subcontinent
  3. Warmest ocean, heavily influenced by monsoon winds.
  4. Key for global oil transport and trade between East and West.
  5. Contains diverse ecosystems, including coral reefs and deep-sea trenches.



SOUTHERN OCEAN

  1. Surrounds Antarctica and is the fourth-largest ocean..
  2. Defined by the Antarctic Circumpolar Current, the strongest ocean current
  3. Extremely cold, with icebergs and unique marine life like penguins and krill.
  4. Plays a major role in regulating Earth's climate and ocean circulation..
  5. Rich in nutrients, supporting one of the most productive marine ecosystems.



ARCTIC OCEAN

  1. Smallest and shallowest of the five oceans, located around the North Pole./li>
  2. Mostly covered by sea ice, especially in winter..
  3. Surrounded by countries like Russia, Canada, and Norway.
  4. Home to polar bears, seals, and unique cold-water species.
  5. Affected by climate change, with rapidly melting ice and rising temperatures.
